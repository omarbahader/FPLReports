🔄 Transfer Strategy — Ibsen

GW30–GW35 Roadmap · Starting Bank £0.6m · Rank #1 in the World

🏆 Context: Protecting a #1 Rank with Tight Funds

With only £0.6m in the bank, Ibsen's transfer strategy must be highly selective. The Wildcard 2 is the biggest lever available — timing its use around the GW31 blank is the critical decision of the run-in. Every free transfer spent incorrectly risks losing rank to the chasing pack.

🚨 GW31 Blank: 4 Starting Players Affected

Gabriel (ARS), Rice (ARS), Semenyo (MCI), and Haaland (MCI) all blank in GW31. This is the defining planning challenge. Options: (A) ride it out with bench cover from Hill/Wilson, or (B) activate Wildcard 2 for GW32 to start fresh post-blank. Strategy below assumes rolling transfers in free weeks; Wildcard 2 is flagged as the nuclear option if rank slips.

Nuclear Option: Wildcard 2

⚡ Wildcard 2 — Recommended Activation: GW32

If the GW31 blank costs significant rank (likely with 4 starters out), activating Wildcard 2 for GW32 allows a complete squad reset heading into DGW33. This is the most powerful tool available and — unlike the other chips — it's still unused. A GW32 activation gives 3 GWs of planning and maximises the value of a fully-optimised DGW33 squad. Key targets to build around: Haaland, Semenyo, B. Fernandes, Virgil, Iwobi, Mac Allister, João Pedro.
